Retention Playbook: How WebEngage and Mailchimp Reduced Churn by 35%

-35% Churn 6 min read Retention · CRM

Acquiring a new customer costs 5–7× more than retaining an existing one. At Stoxboxin, building a proper retention system using WebEngage and Mailchimp reduced customer churn by 35% and generated significant revenue from buyers the brand had written off as lost.

-35%
Customer churn reduction after implementing the full WebEngage + Mailchimp retention system

The Retention Stack

Two tools, clearly defined roles:

  • WebEngage: In-app notifications, push notifications, SMS sequences, and behavioural triggers based on platform actions
  • Mailchimp: Email nurture sequences, win-back campaigns, and segmented broadcast emails

The Win-Back Campaign

The highest-ROI campaign we ran was targeting customers who had purchased but not returned in 60+ days. The sequence:

  • Day 0 (of win-back trigger): Email: "We miss you" — personalised with their last purchase + a relevant new product recommendation
  • Day 3: WebEngage push notification with time-limited 10% discount — exclusive, not available on site
  • Day 7: SMS with a single-link "come back" offer — short, direct, no fluff
  • Day 14: Final email — asking for feedback if they don't return

"Most win-back campaigns fail because they offer a discount too early. Lead with value and personalisation first. Save the discount for the 3rd touchpoint."

Behavioural Triggers That Reduced Churn

  • Low engagement trigger: User hasn't logged in for 14 days → push notification with new content or feature highlight
  • Milestone trigger: User hits 30-day mark → congratulations email with loyalty reward
  • Price drop trigger: Item in user's wishlist drops in price → immediate push notification with direct link
  • Cart abandonment: 30 minutes after abandonment → WhatsApp message + email within the hour
40%
Win-back rate from the 14-day re-engagement sequence for lapsed customers (60+ days inactive)

Segmentation: The Foundation of Everything

Sending the same message to your entire list is the fastest way to increase unsubscribes and decrease deliverability. Segment at minimum by: purchase recency (RFM), category purchased, platform behaviour, and geography. The more personalised the sequence, the higher the engagement — consistently.
