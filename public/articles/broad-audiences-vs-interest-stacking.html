Why Broad Audiences Beat Interest Stacking in 2024

+34% Lower CPA 5 min read Meta Ads

For years the Meta playbook said: stack interests, layer behaviours, narrow the audience. It felt smart. It felt controlled. It was wrong — at least for every D2C brand I ran campaigns on.

After managing campaigns across TruNativ and Aayush Wellness with combined spend in the crores, one finding kept repeating itself: broad audiences with great creative consistently outperformed interest-stacked audiences on CPA.

-34%
Average CPA drop after moving from interest stacking to broad targeting

What Interest Stacking Actually Does Wrong

Layering interests like "fitness enthusiast + supplement buyer + yoga lover" sounds like precision. In reality it:

  • Severely limits audience size — raising CPM automatically
  • Leaves the algorithm with no room to find your real buyers
  • Trusts your assumptions over Meta's actual purchase-intent data
  • Creates audience fatigue 3–4× faster than broad campaigns

Why Broad Works Better

Broad targeting with no interest layer lets Meta's algorithm optimise across the full eligible pool using Pixel signals, customer lists, and creative engagement patterns. It sounds reckless — it isn't.

"Meta already knows more about purchase intent than any interest category you can select. Your job is to give it the right creative signal — not the right demographic guardrail."

The Test That Changed My Approach

Interest Stack Result

Higher CPM, faster fatigue, limited volume. CPA held or rose within 10 days.

Broad Result

Lower CPM, algorithm learned faster. CPA dropped 20–40% within 2 weeks consistently.

Creative Becomes the Targeting Filter

When you go broad, the hook in your creative IS your targeting. "Struggling to hit 4× ROAS?" self-selects performance marketers. "This cleared my skin in 7 days" self-selects skincare buyers. The creative qualifies — the algorithm distributes.

  • Lead with a specific customer problem, not a product feature
  • Use your buyer's language, not marketing-speak
  • Run 5–8 creative variants per broad ad set to give the algorithm options
  • Add your customer list as an Advantage+ signal — not a targeting restriction

When Interest Targeting Still Has a Place

  • Niche B2B products where the buyer is a specific professional role
  • Budgets under ₹5,000/day — broad needs volume to learn
  • Retargeting known visitors or CRM lists
₹5K+
Daily budget threshold where broad consistently outperforms interest stacking in D2C

The 5-Step Broad Playbook

  1. CBO campaign, 1–2 broad ad sets, no interest layers
  2. Add Advantage+ Audience with customer list as signal only
  3. Load 5–8 creatives across different pain-point angles
  4. Let it run 7 days minimum before any conclusions
  5. Kill underperforming creatives, scale winners, keep the broad structure intact
