PMax vs Search: When Each Wins and When to Kill Losers Fast

2.1× Higher Conv. 6 min read Google Ads

Performance Max promised to be the future of Google Ads. For some D2C brands it delivered. For others it quietly cannibalized their best Search campaigns while reporting great ROAS on easy, low-funnel conversions that would have happened anyway.

After managing Google Ads at Aayush Wellness and Stoxboxin — brands with very different budgets and objectives — here's the unfiltered breakdown.

2.1×
Higher conversion rate on exact-match Search vs PMax for high-intent B2B keywords

What PMax Actually Does

PMax runs across all Google inventory — Search, Display, YouTube, Gmail, Maps, Discover — from one campaign. You provide creative assets and audience signals. The algorithm decides everything else. The promise: find your best buyers across all surfaces. The risk: you lose keyword-level visibility and budget control.

When PMax Wins

  • D2C e-commerce with a strong product feed — Shopping placements drive volume when your catalogue and prices are competitive
  • Brands with 50+ monthly conversions — the algorithm needs data; below this threshold it guesses
  • Retargeting at scale — PMax re-engages across YouTube, Display, and Gmail simultaneously
  • Broad geographic expansion — PMax finds buyer clusters in locations you wouldn't have targeted manually

"PMax is a multiplier. If your Search campaigns are broken, PMax amplifies broken. Fix the foundation first."

When Search Wins

  • High-intent B2B keywords — competitor conquesting and branded terms need exact-match control
  • Budgets under ₹10K/day — PMax burns exploring; Search is predictable
  • When you need keyword-level data — PMax hides search terms; Search shows you exactly what converts
  • New accounts with no conversion history — build Search data first, then layer PMax

PMax Best For

D2C shopping, retargeting at scale, brands with 50+ monthly conversions and ₹15K+ daily budget.

Search Best For

B2B intent keywords, new campaigns, tight budgets, keyword-level ROAS control.

The Cannibalization Problem

PMax takes priority over Search when both run simultaneously. Your carefully built Search campaign for "buy whey protein Mumbai" may be starved of budget while PMax blows spend on Display inventory. Signs this is happening:

  • Search Impression Share drops after PMax launch
  • Total conversions don't increase despite higher total spend
  • CPA on Search spikes while PMax reports low CPA
-40%
Average Search Impression Share drop when PMax launches without negative keyword segmentation

How to Run Both Without Cannibalization

  1. Add best-converting Search keywords as Brand Exclusions at account level
  2. Segment PMax by product category — not one account-wide campaign
  3. Monitor Search IS weekly — if it drops, investigate before assuming PMax is performing
  4. Use PMax for prospecting, Search for high-intent bottom-of-funnel

When to Kill PMax — 30-Day Kill Criteria

  • Search IS dropping with no overall conversion increase
  • ROAS lower than standalone Search after 30 days
  • Asset group quality scores stuck at "Poor"
  • CPA climbing week-over-week with no plateau
