GA4 Attribution: Why Your Conversion Data Is Lying to You

Attribution Truth 6 min read Analytics · GA4

Most brands are optimising their campaigns based on data that's wrong. Not slightly wrong — structurally wrong. GA4's default attribution model and iOS privacy changes combine to create a picture of your marketing performance that can be off by 30–50%. Here's how to fix it.

50%
How much conversion data can be underreported in GA4 without server-side tracking and correct attribution setup

The Three Attribution Problems

Problem 1: iOS Signal Loss

Since iOS 14.5, Apple requires apps to ask permission before tracking. The majority of iPhone users opt out. This means Meta's Pixel can't see conversions from iOS users — typically 30–40% of your audience. What gets reported: a fraction of real conversions. What the algorithm optimises on: incomplete data.

Problem 2: Last-Click Default

GA4's default attribution gives 100% credit to the last touchpoint before conversion. A customer who saw your Meta ad, searched your brand on Google, clicked a Google Ad, and then converted — all credit goes to Google. Meta gets zero. Your data shows Google is more efficient. Your reality is different.

Problem 3: Cross-Device Gaps

A user sees your Meta ad on their phone, considers it for two days, then converts on their laptop. Unless you have a logged-in user system or Meta's Conversions API set up, this looks like two separate users — the ad gets no credit for the conversion.

"Data-driven attribution in GA4 + Meta Conversions API (CAPI) is the minimum viable setup for any brand spending over ₹1L/month. Without it, you're flying blind."

The Correct Attribution Setup

  • Switch GA4 to Data-Driven Attribution: Go to GA4 → Admin → Attribution Settings → Data-Driven. This distributes credit across all touchpoints based on actual conversion paths.
  • Set Up Meta Conversions API (CAPI): Server-side tracking that sends conversion events directly from your server to Meta — bypassing iOS blocking. Typically recovers 20–40% of "lost" conversions.
  • Enable Enhanced Conversions in Google Ads: Hashed first-party data improves match rates for cross-device and cross-session conversions.
  • Use UTM Parameters Consistently: Every ad, every email, every influencer link needs proper UTM tags so GA4 knows exactly where traffic came from.

What to Trust When Data Conflicts

Meta's reporting will always show higher numbers than GA4. Google will always attribute more credit to Search. The source of truth: your payment gateway or Shopify order dashboard. Work backwards from actual orders to validate which channel's attribution model most closely matches reality.
