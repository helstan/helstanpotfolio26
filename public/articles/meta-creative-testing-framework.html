The 5-Creative Testing Framework That Cut Our CPA in Half

-50% CPA 5 min read Meta · Creative

Most brands A/B test creatives the wrong way — two variations, different audiences, different times, different budgets, drawing conclusions from noise. This framework put structure around creative testing and cut CPA by 50% in 6 weeks.

-50%
CPA reduction across D2C Meta campaigns using the 5-creative testing framework

Why Most Creative Tests Fail

  • Testing too many variables at once — you can't know what moved the needle
  • Insufficient budget per variant — each creative needs minimum ₹500–1,000/day to gather signal
  • Killing tests too early — 3-day data is noise; 7-day data starts to mean something
  • No clear kill criteria defined before the test begins

The Framework: 5 Hooks, 1 Body, 1 CTA

Isolate the hook as your only variable. Keep the body (problem/solution) and CTA identical across all 5 variants. The hook — first 2–3 seconds — drives 80–90% of CTR variance. Finding the winning hook is worth more than any other optimisation.

The 5 Hook Types to Test

Pain statement · Bold claim · Social proof · Curiosity gap · How-to / Tutorial opening

Kill vs. Scale Rule

After 7 days: CPM-to-CPA ratio above account average → kill. 20%+ better CPA → scale budget 2×.

Budget Allocation for Testing

Run all 5 variants inside a single CBO ad set at ₹1,500–2,000/day. Let Meta's delivery algorithm distribute spend based on early signals. Don't manually equalise budget between creatives — that defeats the algorithm.

Reading the Signals

  • Day 1–3: Watch CTR and CPM only. Don't make decisions.
  • Day 4–5: Check Add-to-Cart or Lead rate. One or two variants should be pulling ahead.
  • Day 6–7: Look at CPA. Kill anything more than 30% above your target CPA. Scale anything 20%+ below.
  • Week 2: Take your winning hook. Now test 5 body variations. Repeat the cycle.

What This Looks Like in Practice

For a wellness brand running Meta campaigns, we ran this framework across 3 consecutive cycles over 6 weeks. Each cycle found a new winning angle that cut CPA further. By cycle 3, CPA was 52% lower than the starting baseline — from ₹420 to ₹201 per purchase.
